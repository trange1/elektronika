Limit Operators

To evaluate the limit of a symbolic expression:

  1. Press [Ctrl] L to create the limit operator, or click on the Calculus toolbar.

  2. Enter the expression for which you want to compute the limit in the placeholder to the right of "lim."
  3. Enter the limiting variable in the placeholder to the left of the arrow below the "lim."
  4. Enter the limiting value in the placeholder to the right of the arrow below the "lim."
  5. Press [Spacebar] until the entire expression is between the editing lines.
  6. Press [Ctrl] [.] or click the right arrow on the Symbolic toolbar to insert the symbolic equal sign.
  7. Click outside the expression or press [Enter].

Note: You cannot evaluate limits using the numerical equal = sign.

If the limit does not exist, Mathcad returns an error message.

You can also evaluate one-sided limits symbolically:
  • To insert a limit from the left, press [Ctrl] [Shift] B or click on the Calculus toolbar.

  • To insert a limit from the right, press [Ctrl] [Shift] A or click on the Calculus toolbar.
